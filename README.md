# LR2HR
Automated crop recognition in UAV-based precision agriculture is frequently hindered by non-uniform illumination and dense canopy shadows, which obscure the critical morphological and spectral signatures necessary for species differentiation. To address these challenges without the domain-shift limitations of data-driven models, this paper proposes a physics-based enhancement framework via multi-scale frequency decoupling and bidirectional illumination reshaping. Unlike conventional single-scale mapping, our approach leverages Gaussian pyramids to decompose imagery into distinct spatial frequencies, enabling a variational Retinex model to adaptively balance illumination and reflectance at each scale. This decoupling strategy effectively suppresses halos and artifacts that typically degrade the structural integrity of crop features in single-scale methods. To resolve local exposure imbalances, a dual-path boosting mechanism is implemented to recover low-contrast textures in shadowed areas while strictly preserving color fidelity in highlights—a prerequisite for reliable vegetation index calculation and downstream classification. Experimental results on real-world agricultural datasets demonstrate that the proposed method achieves an NIQE of 1.81 and a PIQE of 4.81. By restoring obscured high-frequency details and maintaining spectral consistency, this algorithm provides a robust, zero-shot preprocessing solution that significantly improves the reliability of crop recognition under complex field lighting conditions. This repository provides the official implementation of the paper "A Multi-Scale Decoupling Strategy for UAV Image Enhancement via Dual-Illumination Boosting“
